Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this website is to help any and all of those who have always wanted to have an everyday japanese. This site will help them have the basics of the language, a foundation that they will develop by studying and applying some basic everyday grammar and vocabulary.

Audience

This site is for anyone, young or old, who wants to learn the basics of the Japanese language, perhaps for a trip, studies, job, or even as a hobby. The site is for those who haven't much time to spend looking for materials to study or courses to take. It is for even those who can't afford a paid course. They can learn the basics of Japanese for free from their computers or mobile devices.

Content

Home page

Welcome to Everyday Japanese!

Our purpose is to help you build a solid foundation in Japanese by learning keywords and basic grammar in a very natural and simple way that will help you hold an everyday conversation in Japanese. If you've ever been one of those who have spent hours in front of a computer doing research to understand a concept or have a question solved, or if you're just tired of taking paid courses or you can't even afford one.

We help you better organize your studies by providing different ways to study the contents of the site. You can choose to study by topic, if you want to learn through examples in certain situations like conversations at home, school, work, market, mall, hotel, airport, etc., you can choose to study that way. You can also study by vocabulary topics like food, human body parts, transportation, clothing, etc., and grammatical structures like pronouns, articles, possessives, verb conjugation, etc.

The site is full of examples for each topic, grammar, and vocabulary, so you can have a more stimulating and inspiring experience.

Before you really get started studying all of Japanese, something very important that you should be aware of is that there are Japanese proficiency levels as well as a world-well-known and recognized test to measure the proficiency level of non-native learners of Japanese. It is called JLPT, Japanese Language Proficiency Test. The JLPT has five levels: N1, N2, N3, N4, and N5. The easiest level is N5, and the most difficult level is N1. N4 and N5 measure the level of understanding of basic Japanese mainly learned in class. N1 and N2 measure the level of understanding of Japanese used in a broad range of scenes in actual everyday life. N3 is a bridging level between N1/N2 and N4/N5.

The focus of this site is to help you have an N4 Japanese.

We wish you the best on your journey, happy studying!

N5 Vocabulary list

This is a list of the vocabulary that you need to know in order to reach the JLPT N5. In total, you will need to have a vocabulary of about 600 words.
This list includes lessons for the most common and important words for you to know, all listed in alphabetical order.

N5 Grammar List

This is a complete list of the Japanese grammar rules needed to study for and reach the N5 JLPT level. Although there is no official JLPT N5 文法 grammar list, most grammar points that have been featured on previous tests are included in this list. Each lesson is arranged in English romaji alphabetical order. In total, there are over 80 grammar lessons that you need to know in order to reach the N5.

N5 Kanji List

There are about 80 kanji in total that you will need to know in order to reach the N5, and we have them all listed here with each one linking to a customized lesson., The kanji are listed in order of most frequently used, with the most common characters listed first. Click on any of the kanji below to go to the lesson for that kanji. There you can get more detailed information, including common vocabulary words and example sentences using that kanji.
